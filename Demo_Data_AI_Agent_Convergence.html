What this is

This proof of concept demonstrates that an AI agent — connected to a privacy platform via the MCP (Model Context Protocol) standard — can continuously monitor, detect, analyse, and escalate data and privacy operations, with full data & privacy governance guardrails and human oversight at every consequential step.

Governance Framework — Why Is It Safe to Deploy

The AI agent operates under a strict governance framework designed for enterprise privacy contexts. Every action is governed by Privacy by Design principles — meaning governance is built into the architecture, not added as an afterthought. A human in the loop checkpoint gates every consequential action: no email is sent, no data deleted, no calendar entry created without explicit human approval. The agent applies data minimisation at every step — reading only what is necessary and retaining no raw personal data values. Every action is recorded in a full audit trail with timestamp, access scope, and guardrail applied. Purpose limitation confines the agent strictly to privacy operations — it cannot act outside its defined scope. The result: AI that is not just capable, but accountable and auditable.

What Is Demonstrated
Source systems scanned:  Salesforce CRM, Workday HR, Oracle HCM — 32 personal data fields classified across basic, sensitive, and special category tiers.
Scenario 1 — Personal data discovery:  Agent detected health data (special category) sitting in a CRM system where it should not be. Regulatory obligations under APP 3, GDPR Art.9, and the NDB scheme were identified. No data deleted without human approval.
Scenario 2 — DSR management:  Agent identified 3 overdue data subject requests — one 26 days past deadline — drafted an escalation and waited for human approval before sending.
Scenario 3 — Risk monitoring:  Agent scored and prioritised 8 open privacy risks, producing a DPO briefing with regulatory references and recommended actions. No risk records were modified.
Scenario 4 — Assessment deadlines:  Agent tracked 6 DPIAs, detected a workload conflict and a cross-register dependency chain that no single human scanning separate spreadsheets would reliably notice.
